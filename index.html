A Brief Performance History of Gianna Read-Skelton

Introduction

Gianna Read-Skelton is a triple threat performer with credits in Opera, Theatre, and Dance. She holds a Bachelor of Music in Opera and Vocal Performance from the University of Alberta, as well as a Theatre Arts Diploma from MacEwan University. She has been working professionally in theatre for nearly 10 years, but has been performing nearly her whole life.

Opera

Gianna began her classical music studies in Calgary while in high school and decided to pursue them in her post-secondary education. She attended the University of Alberta for Music where she studied under mezzo-soprano Elizabeth Turnbull and with collaborative pianist Shannon Hiebert. While at the University of Alberta, she performed multiple solo concerts, featured in many operas and operettas, travelled and sung with the Madrigal Singers Choir under the direction of Leonard Ratzlaff at Koerner Hall in Toronto as well as the Winspear Centre in Edmonton. Along with her studies in Edmonton, she also attended summer opera festivals including the Halifax Summer Opera Festival, the Yorkshire Opera Festival in England, and the Centre for Opera Studies in Italy.

Edmonton Theatre

Following her classical training, Gianna attended MacEwan University for Theatre Arts to pursue a career in musical theatre. She studied multiple styles of dance, classical and modern acting, and furthered her vocal training in the Broadway style. After graduating from MacEwan, she began to work professionally in Edmonton at the Varscona Theatre and the Mayfield Dinner Theatre. She also participated multiple years in the Edmonton International Fringe Festival, the second largest Fringe Festival in the world.

Calgary Theatre

After moving back to her hometown of Calgary, Gianna continued her performance career in the local theatres. She mentored and performed with StoryBook Theatre, Honest Collective Theatre, and most recently performed with The Shakespeare Company. Upcoming, you can find her onstage at Stage West Dinner Theatre from February to April 2025 in the Canadian farce, "Too Many Cooks" by Marcia Kash and Douglas E. Hughes.
